# ACME TRAVEL INSURANCE

## Trip Cancellation Coverage - Policy Terms & Conditions

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024 **Version:** 3.2

---

## 1. COVERAGE OVERVIEW

### 1.1 What is Covered

This policy provides reimbursement for prepaid, non-refundable trip costs if you
must cancel your covered trip due to a covered reason listed in Section 2.

**Maximum Coverage:** Up to the lesser of:

- 100% of insured trip cost, or
- $50,000 per person

### 1.2 Who is Covered

- Primary policyholder
- Immediate family members traveling with policyholder
- Travel companions listed on the policy

---

## 2. COVERED CANCELLATION REASONS

### 2.1 Medical Reasons

Trip cancellation is covered if you, a traveling companion, or an immediate
family member:

- Becomes seriously ill or injured requiring hospitalization
- Dies
- Is diagnosed with a communicable disease requiring quarantine
- Experiences a pregnancy complication requiring bed rest

**Required Documentation:** Medical certificate from licensed physician

### 2.2 Employment Reasons

Coverage applies if you or a traveling companion:

- Is involuntarily terminated (layoff, not for cause)
- Is required to work due to emergency (healthcare, emergency services, utility
  workers)
- Is transferred more than 100 miles from current location

**Required Documentation:** Letter from employer on company letterhead

### 2.3 Natural Disasters & Severe Weather

Coverage applies if:

- Your primary residence becomes uninhabitable due to fire, flood, or natural
  disaster
- A hurricane, earthquake, or named storm makes destination inaccessible
- Travel supplier (airline/hotel) ceases operations due to financial insolvency

**Required Documentation:** Government emergency declaration or supplier
bankruptcy filing

### 2.4 Legal Obligations

Coverage applies if you:

- Are subpoenaed or required for jury duty
- Are called to active military duty
- Experience theft of passport or visa within 10 days of departure

**Required Documentation:** Court summons, military orders, or police report

---

## 3. EXCLUSIONS - WHAT IS NOT COVERED

### 3.1 The following are NOT covered

- Cancellation due to fear of travel or "change of mind"
- Pre-existing medical conditions (unless waiver purchased)
- Mental health conditions (unless resulting in hospitalization)
- Voluntary job loss, resignation, or retirement
- Cancellation due to business financial reasons
- Pregnancy in final trimester (after week 28)
- Travel booked within 48 hours of departure
- Adventure sports injuries (skydiving, bungee jumping, etc.)

### 3.2 Excluded Destinations

- Travel to countries under official government travel warnings
- Travel to high-risk conflict zones

---

## 4. CLAIMS PROCESS

### 4.1 When to File

Claims must be filed:

- Within 90 days of cancellation date
- Before originally scheduled departure date, when possible

### 4.2 Required Documentation (All Claims)

1. Completed claim form with policy number
2. Original trip booking confirmation/receipt
3. Cancellation confirmation from travel provider
4. Proof of non-refundable amount
5. Supporting documentation for cancellation reason (see Section 2)

### 4.3 Optional but Recommended

- Email correspondence with travel provider
- Bank/credit card statements showing payment
- Itemized cost breakdown

### 4.4 Claims Review Timeline

- Initial review: 5-7 business days
- Additional information requests: 3-5 business days for response
- Final decision: Within 30 days of receiving complete documentation
- Payment: Within 15 days of approval

---

## 5. COVERAGE LIMITS & DEDUCTIBLES

### 5.1 Coverage Limits

- Maximum per person: $50,000
- Maximum per policy: $100,000 (family coverage)
- Trip duration: Maximum 180 days

### 5.2 Deductibles

- Standard: $250 per person
- Family: $500 maximum per policy
- Waived if hospitalization exceeds 48 hours

---

## 6. POLICY PURCHASE REQUIREMENTS

### 6.1 When to Purchase

Policy must be purchased:

- Within 15 days of initial trip deposit, OR
- Before final payment date

### 6.2 Trip Cost Calculation

Insured amount must equal:

- All prepaid, non-refundable trip costs
- Including: flights, accommodation, tours, transfers, event tickets
- Excluding: Travel insurance premium, meals not prepaid

---

## 7. PRE-EXISTING CONDITION WAIVER

### 7.1 Eligibility

Pre-existing conditions are covered if you:

- Purchase policy within 15 days of initial trip deposit
- Insure 100% of prepaid trip costs
- Are medically able to travel at time of purchase

### 7.2 Definition of Pre-Existing Condition

A condition for which medical advice, diagnosis, care, or treatment was
recommended or received during the 60 days immediately preceding policy
purchase.
